    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{drc_2015_dco_lasso_coefficients.png}
    \caption{Coefficients, DRC 2015  \begin{itemize}
      \item The explanatory power of the variables is modest, with deviance ranging from 25.07 to 48.27\% across data strata.
      \item Most included variables have median coefficient of zero or credible intervals that span 0.
      \item However, most variables have skewed credible intervals that are bounded away from zero, suggesting modest but consistent effect.
      \item Effect size of regions and consultation language is large, but significance needs more context. (see attempt at hierarchical model); 
      \item Of note, for patients in second trimester, male HCWs were associated with longer consultation time (ref female HCWs).
      \item In follow-up consultations, male HCWs are associated with decreased length, regardless of the trimester.
      \item Compared with doctors, nurses were associated with increased appointment length in second trimester, first ANC and decreased length in third trimester, first ANC
        (CrI spans zero in follow-up for both trimesters).
    \end{itemize}}
  \label{fig:drc 2015 dco lm}
\end{figure}

  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{drc_midline_dco_lasso_coefficients.png}
  \caption{DRC midline survey \begin{itemize}
    \item The explanatory power of the variables is low, with deviance ranging from 0 to 46.7\% across data strata.
    \item All included variables have median coefficient of zero.
    \item However, most variables have skewed credible intervals that are bounded away from zero, suggesting modest but consistent effect.
    \item Of note, for patients in second trimester having their first consultation, male HCWs were associated with smaller consultation time (ref female HCWs).
    \item In follow-up consultations, male HCWs are associated with increased length, regardless of the trimester.
    \item Compared with doctors, nurses were associated with increased appointment length in second trimester, first ANC and decreased length in third trimester, first ANC
      (CrI spans zero in follow-up for both trimesters).
    \item Strong regional differences (see estimates for province and language), but significance needs more context.
    \end{itemize}}
